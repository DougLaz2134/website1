Famous Artist Trivia

Leonardo da Vinci: Not only a painter but also an engineer, scientist, and inventor. His notebooks contain designs for flying machines and detailed anatomical sketches.
Michelangelo: Considered himself more a sculptor than a painter and was initially reluctant to paint the Sistine Chapel ceiling.
Vincent van Gogh: Painted over 900 works in just 10 years, yet only sold one painting during his lifetime.
Pablo Picasso: Could draw before he could talk. His first word was “pencil,” and he completed his first painting at age nine.
Salvador Dalí: Designed the Chupa Chups logo, a fact often overlooked despite his prominence in Surrealist art.
Fernando Amorsolo: The first National Artist of the Philippines, known for luminous paintings of rural Filipino life and idealized portraits of women.
Juan Luna: Renowned for his painting Spoliarium, which won a gold medal at the 1884 Madrid Exposition of Fine Arts.
Carlos “Botong” Francisco: Revived the art of mural painting in the Philippines with epic historical pieces considered national treasures.
Benedicto Cabrera (BenCab): Declared National Artist for Visual Arts in 2006, known for expressive portrayals of the Filipino figure, especially “Sabel.”
Anita Magsaysay-Ho: The only female member of the “Thirteen Moderns,” known for dynamic depictions of Filipino women at work.
